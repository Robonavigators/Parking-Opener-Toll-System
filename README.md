 What are toll gates ?
 Toll gates, also known as toll booths or toll plazas, are points on roads or bridges where drivers must pay a fee to use the road or bridge. These fees, known as tolls, are used to fund the construction, maintenance, and improvement of the road infrastructure.
Drivers can pay the toll using cash, debit/credit cards, or electronic payment systems like Fastag.
Toll gates are barriers, often at the entrance or exit of a toll road or bridge, where drivers are required to pay a fee to use the road or bridge
Toll gates are commonly found on expressways, motorways, and bridges where toll fees are charged for their use.
